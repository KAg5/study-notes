### **1. Basic Structural Rules (Polarity & Tense)**

The most fundamental topic in tag questions is the rule of **Opposite Polarity**. A positive statement requires a negative tag, and a negative statement requires a positive tag. The tense of the tag must always match the tense of the main verb in the statement.

- **Logic:** To confirm a positive fact, we ask for a negative contradiction; to confirm a negative fact, we ask for a positive contradiction.
    
- **Key Points:**
    
    - **Auxiliary Match:** If the sentence uses "is," "are," "have," or "will," the tag must use the same. (e.g., _She **is** late, **isn't** she?_)
        
    - **Implicit Auxiliaries:** In the Simple Present or Past (where no auxiliary is visible), we use _do, does,_ or _did_. (e.g., _He **plays** well, **doesn't** he?_)
        
    - **Contracted Forms:** Negative tags must always use contractions (e.g., _won't_ instead of _will not_).

### **2. Imperatives & Suggestions (Commands & "Let’s")**

Imperative sentences (commands, requests, or invitations) do not follow the standard polarity rule. Instead, they use specific modal verbs to adjust the tone of the request or suggestion.

- **Logic:** Since imperatives are directed at "you," the tag focuses on the listener's willingness or the group's collective action.
    
- **Key Points:**
    
    - **Standard Imperatives:** For most commands or requests, the tag is **"will you?"** regardless of whether the statement is positive or negative. (e.g., _Close the door, **will you?**_ or _Don't be late, **will you?**_)
        
    - **Suggestions with "Let’s":** Sentences beginning with "Let's" (Let us) always take the tag **"shall we?"** to seek collective agreement. (e.g., _Let’s go for a walk, **shall we?**_)
        
    - **Polite Invitations:** Sometimes **"won't you?"** is used for polite invitations. (e.g., _Take a seat, **won't you?**_)

### **3. Special Pronoun Subjects (Indefinite & Demonstrative)**

When the subject of the sentence is an indefinite pronoun (like _everyone, nobody_) or a demonstrative pronoun (like _this, that_), the pronoun in the tag must be adjusted to maintain grammatical logic.

- **Logic:** Indefinite pronouns referring to people are treated as plural in the tag, while those referring to things are treated as singular.
    
- **Key Points:**
    
    - **Plural People:** For _someone, everyone, nobody, anyone,_ the tag pronoun is always **"they."** (e.g., _Nobody called, did **they**?_)
        
    - **Singular Things:** For _something, everything, nothing,_ the tag pronoun is **"it."** (e.g., _Everything is ready, isn't **it**?_)
        
    - **This/That vs. These/Those:** "This/That" becomes **"it"** in the tag, while "These/Those" becomes **"they."** (e.g., _That is your car, isn't **it**?_)

### **4. Words with Inherent Negative Meaning**

A sentence can be "negative" even if it doesn't contain the word "not." Words that imply rarity or absence make the statement negative, requiring a **positive tag**.

- **Logic:** Because the meaning of the sentence is already negative, a negative tag would create an incorrect "double negative" effect.
    
- **Key Points:**
    
    - **Adverbs of Frequency:** Words like _seldom, hardly, rarely, scarcely,_ and _barely_ trigger a positive tag. (e.g., _She **rarely** visits, **does** she?_)
        
    - **Negative Quantifiers:** Words like _few_ and _little_ (without the article 'a') are negative. (e.g., _Few people knew, **did** they?_)
        
    - **Note on 'A few/A little':** These are considered positive and take a negative tag. (e.g., _A few people knew, **didn't** they?_)

### **5. The "I am" Exception & Modal Tags**

There are specific irregular forms in English tag questions, most notably the "I am" construction, and rules regarding modal verbs like _can, should,_ and _must_.

- **Logic:** Some standard grammatical forms (like _amn't I_) do not exist in standard English, necessitating a substitute.
    
- **Key Points:**
    
    - **The "I am" Rule:** The tag for "I am" is **"aren't I?"** (e.g., _I am right, **aren't I?**_). However, the negative statement "I am not" follows the standard rule: _I am not late, **am I?**_
        
    - **Modal Consistency:** If a modal verb is used in the main clause, it must be repeated in the tag. (e.g., _You **can** swim, **can't** you?_ or _We **should** go, **shouldn't** we?_)
        
    - **"Used to":** This semi-modal usually takes a "did" tag. (e.g., _He used to smoke, **didn't** he?_)

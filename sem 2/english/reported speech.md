### 1. Tense Backshift Mechanism

When the reporting verb is in the past tense (e.g., _said_, _told_), the verbs within the inverted commas must move one step back into the past to maintain chronological accuracy.

- **Shift:** Direct present tenses transform into their corresponding past forms to reflect the time elapsed since the original statement.
    
- **Key Rules:**
    
    - **Simple Present to Past:** "I eat" becomes "He said he ate."
        
    - **Present Continuous to Past Continuous:** "I am eating" becomes "He said he was eating."
        
    - **Present Perfect to Past Perfect:** "I have eaten" becomes "He said he had eaten."
        
    - **Exception:** No backshift occurs if the statement expresses a universal truth or a habitual fact (e.g., "The sun rises in the east").
        

**Recall Chain:** Shift → Simple → Continuous → Exception

### 2. Pronominal and Possessive Adjustments

Pronouns must change to reflect the perspective of the person reporting the speech rather than the person who originally spoke.

- **Logic:** The speaker's "I" usually becomes "he" or "she" when someone else relates the story to a third party.
    
- **Key Rules:**
    
    - **First Person:** Changes according to the subject of the reporting verb (e.g., _I_ $\rightarrow$ _He/She_).
        
    - **Second Person:** Changes according to the object of the reporting verb (e.g., _You_ $\rightarrow$ _Me/Him/Her_).
        
    - **Third Person:** Remains unchanged (e.g., _He, She, They_ stay as they are).
        

**Recall Chain:** Logic → First → Second → Third

---

### 3. Deictic Expressions (Time and Place)

Words pointing to a specific time or location in the speaker's immediate environment must be altered to fit the reporter's context.

- **Transformation:** Words indicating "nearness" are replaced by words indicating "distance" in space and time.
    
- **Key Rules:**
    
    - **Place:** "Here" becomes "there"; "this" becomes "that."
        
    - **Time (Immediate):** "Now" becomes "then"; "today" becomes "that day."
        
    - **Time (Relative):** "Tomorrow" becomes "the next day"; "yesterday" becomes "the previous day."
        

**Recall Chain:** Transformation → Place → Immediate → Relative

---

### 4. Interrogative Transformations (Reporting Questions)

Reporting a question requires changing the sentence structure from interrogative to assertive (statement) form.

- **Structure:** The question mark is removed, and the verb follows the subject instead of preceding it.
    
- **Key Rules:**
    
    - **Yes/No Questions:** These are introduced using the connectors "if" or "whether" (e.g., "Are you coming?" $\rightarrow$ He asked _if_ I was coming).
        
    - **Wh- Questions:** The specific question word (Who, What, Why) acts as the connector; no extra "that" or "if" is used.
        
    - **Removal:** Do/Does/Did auxiliaries used for questioning are dropped in the reported statement.
        

**Recall Chain:** Structure → Yes/No → Wh- → Removal

---

### 5. Imperatives and Exclamations

Commands, requests, and emotional outbursts require specific reporting verbs that convey the tone of the original utterance.

- **Logic:** Instead of using "that," imperatives use the infinitive (to + verb) to link the reporting clause and the reported action.
    
- **Key Rules:**
    
    - **Commands/Requests:** "Said to" changes to _ordered, requested,_ or _advised_ followed by "to" (e.g., "Go away" $\rightarrow$ He _ordered_ me _to_ go away).
        
    - **Negative Imperatives:** "Don't" becomes "not to."
        
    - **Exclamations:** These are reported using "exclaimed with joy/sorrow" followed by "that" and a statement form (e.g., "Alas!" $\rightarrow$ He _exclaimed with sorrow that_ he was ruined).
